## Learning goals

By the end of Session 10, every student can:
- **Present** their pet: what it is, what it does, and one thing they added.
- **Reflect** on what they learned and what was hard.
- **Give and receive** kind, useful feedback on a peer's project.
- Leave knowing **how to keep going** if they want to.

## 1. Showcase — present your pet

Each student (or pair) gives a short demo using the **demo path they rehearsed in Session 9**.
A simple structure to offer them:

> *"Meet **[name]**, my pet. It can **[core thing]**. I added **[your feature]** — watch.
> The hardest part was **[X]**, and I learned **[Y]**."*

Keep each demo to **1–2 minutes** with one audience question. Remind presenters: if a live
feature misbehaves, fall back to the plan from Session 9 (a backup step, a screenshot) and keep
going — that's exactly what real demos do.

**Peer feedback (the 20' block):** watching a neighbour's demo up close, each student gives one
**glow** (something genuinely good) and one **grow** (a question or an idea for "what next").

---

## 2. Reflect — look back at the journey

Short written or spoken reflection. Prompts:
- Which fundamental clicked most for you — variables, functions, `if`, loops, saving, or the
  mood ladder?
- What are you proudest of building?
- What was the hardest bug, and how did you crack it?
- What's one thing about coding you understand now that you didn't in Session 1?
- If you had one more week, what would you add?

*(This is the concept of the day: consolidating the learning by putting it into your own words.)*

---

## 3. Celebrate + what's next *(closing)*

- **Recognition** for every pet — including the baseline ones. Finishing the course *is* the
  achievement; a working pet you understand beats a flashy one you don't.
- **The map, full circle.** Put the Session 1 "meet the team" map back on screen: *"Remember
  when this looked impossible? You've now worked in every one of these."*
- **How to keep going:** they keep the device and the repo; point them at the track menu
  (Session 7) and the design notes for the big ideas (ESP-NOW, evolution, a web dashboard) so
  the tinkering can continue after the course.

---

## Teacher notes

- **Strict, kind timekeeping.** 1–2 minutes each, or the showcase overruns. A visible timer helps.
- **Make every demo safe to give.** Celebrate baseline-pet demos as warmly as ambitious ones;
  no student should feel their project "doesn't count."
- **Capture it.** Photograph/screen-record the pets and demos — great for the program's record
  and for the students to keep.
- **Have a fallback for tech failures.** WiFi/ESP-NOW demos can fail in a crowded room; the
  Session 9 fallback plan is what saves the moment.
- **No device build is required from students today** — but have spare charged devices on hand.
